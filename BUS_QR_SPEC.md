# Charon Bus QR Specification

## Document Status

- Status: Draft v1
- Date: 2026-03-08
- Purpose: Define the durable bus QR model, boarding authorization rules, physical and operational UX, security controls, and fraud-resistance behavior for the Charon platform.

## 1. Design Intent

The bus QR system exists to identify the physical bus being boarded without forcing the driver to hold out a phone during boarding.

Core principle:

- QR proves `which bus`
- schedule-backed service window proves `whether boarding is currently valid`
- optional on-device location check helps warn the rider about likely bad scans

The QR itself is not sufficient to authorize payment.

## 2. Why This Model

The durable bus QR design replaces driver-held dynamic QR for practical reasons:

- drivers should not need to keep a phone visible during a crowded boarding window
- battery level, screen brightness, and device quality should not control whether students can pay
- a printed or mounted bus sticker is simpler for real campus operations
- bus identity is stable even if route sessions change

This design also addresses two key failure modes:

- `Forgot to End`: a driver forgetting to tap `End Route` must not leave the bus indefinitely chargeable
- `Dorm Room Scan`: a student photographing a sticker and scanning it remotely must not become a low-friction abuse path

## 3. Core Decisions

- One durable QR asset per physical bus.
- QR assets are generated by admins only.
- QR payload includes `institute_id`, `bus_id`, `qr_version`, and a server signature.
- A human-readable short numeric bus code is printed alongside the QR and is always available as a fallback manual entry path.
- Morning and evening runs are separate scheduled service instances.
- Each service instance covers the full round trip, not just one leg.
- One bus is assigned to one route in v1.
- Boarding authorization is schedule-authoritative, not telemetry-authoritative.
- Telemetry helps logistics and monitoring, but lack of fresh telemetry must not block fare payment.
- Driver start and end actions are operational signals, not the only source of boarding truth.

## 4. Physical Asset Model

### 4.1 Bus Asset

Each physical bus must have:

- a stable internal `bus_id`
- a short human-readable `bus_code`
- a durable QR asset
- an active `qr_version`
- an institute-scoped identity

### 4.2 Printed Bus Sticker

The system only generates the QR asset and printable output. Physical placement is the deployer's responsibility.

Printed sticker content should include:

- QR graphic
- institute name or short identifier
- bus code in large text
- optional bus label such as plate or fleet number
- optional route label for operator convenience
- short manual fallback text such as `Enter Bus Code: 1042`

The QR is expected to be durable enough for daily repeated scanning, but placement, lamination, and replacement policy are left to the deployer.

## 5. QR Payload and Signing

### 5.1 Signed Payload

The durable QR payload must contain:

- `institute_id`
- `bus_id`
- `qr_version`
- `asset_type=BUS_QR`
- signature

### 5.2 What the QR Must Not Contain

The QR must not contain:

- active session identifiers
- fare amount
- driver identity
- student identity
- mutable operational state

Those values must be resolved server-side at scan time.

### 5.3 Versioning and Rotation

- Admins can rotate a bus QR by incrementing `qr_version`.
- Old QR versions remain valid for a `1 day` grace period after rotation.
- Any use of an old QR during the grace period must be flagged in the admin dashboard.
- After the grace period, old versions must be rejected.

Rotation reasons may include:

- damaged sticker
- leaked photo
- bus reassignment or replacement
- suspected abuse

## 6. Service Instance Model

### 6.1 Service Instance Definition

A service instance is the schedule-backed boarding window associated with a bus for one morning or evening run.

Rules:

- Morning and evening are separate service instances.
- Each service instance covers the full outbound and return cycle.
- One bus has at most one valid boardable service instance at a time.
- Ad hoc unscheduled service instances are allowed in v1, but admin-created only.

### 6.2 Schedule Authority

Scheduled service instances are the primary source of boarding truth.

For scheduled service instances:

- boarding becomes valid `30 minutes` before scheduled start
- boarding remains valid until `15 minutes` after scheduled end
- scans outside this window must be blocked

Driver actions:

- driver `Start` may mark the service as actively running for operations
- driver `End` may close the service earlier when appropriate
- missing driver `End` must not keep boarding open forever

Because of this, the `Forgot to End` problem is solved by schedule-backed auto-expiry rather than by trusting human behavior.

### 6.3 User-Facing Error for Inactive Services

If a QR is valid but there is no current boardable service instance for the bus, the student app should show:

- `Trip Not Active Yet`

This message is intentionally simple and can cover:

- too early
- too late
- no scheduled run currently active
- route not started for the current window

### 6.4 Driver Attachment to Bus and Service Instance

The same durable bus QR may be used by the driver app, but only as a bus selector after driver authentication.

Rules:

- driver identity comes from the authenticated driver session
- bus identity comes from bus QR or numeric bus code
- scanning the public bus QR does not grant driver privilege by itself
- backend must attach the authenticated driver to the current eligible service instance for that bus
- if there is no eligible service instance, driver attachment must fail clearly
- if the bus has more than one eligible service instance around a valid boundary, the driver may choose manually
- if the bus has conflicting service data beyond a legitimate boundary case, driver attachment must fail and be flagged for admin review

## 7. Boarding Authorization Rules

Boarding is allowed only when all of the following are true:

- QR signature is valid
- institute identifier matches the current deployment
- QR version is current or still inside the rotation grace period
- exactly one boardable service instance exists for the scanned bus
- the student has provided a stop selection
- duplicate-boarding rule is not violated for the student and service instance
- wallet rules, sponsorship rules, or emergency permit rules allow the boarding path

Boarding must be blocked when:

- QR signature is invalid
- bus is unknown
- QR version is expired
- no boardable service instance exists
- multiple boardable service instances exist for the same bus
- request is outside the service window

## 8. Anti-Abuse and Safety Model

### 8.1 Telemetry Is Not a Boarding Gate

Live telemetry must not be used as a hard gate for fare collection.

Reason:

- aggressive Android background killing is common on target devices
- students may board well before the driver is operationally ready
- payment reliability must not depend on telemetry freshness

Telemetry may still be used for:

- admin visibility
- stale-service monitoring
- guardian live view
- alerting

### 8.2 On-Device Location Safety Check

The student app should perform a local location safety check before final charge.

Rules:

- the calculation happens on-device
- raw student GPS coordinates are not sent to the server
- the server receives only the location-check result and related audit flags
- the comparison uses the campus geofence only in v1

Why campus-only geofence:

- the goal is to catch obvious remote scans with low admin setup cost
- stop-level geofence setup is intentionally avoided in v1

### 8.3 Location Outcomes

Possible local location results:

- `INSIDE_CAMPUS_GEOFENCE`
- `OUTSIDE_CAMPUS_GEOFENCE`
- `PERMISSION_DENIED`
- `LOCATION_UNAVAILABLE`
- `LOW_ACCURACY`

The student device should also check accuracy quality.

Required accuracy threshold:

- `20m` or better is considered good

### 8.4 Location UX Rules

If permission is granted and the device appears outside the campus geofence:

- show a dangerous-scan warning
- require extra confirmation
- still allow override

If permission is denied:

- show a warning plus extra confirmation
- still allow boarding attempt

If location is unavailable or low accuracy:

- show a caution state
- still allow boarding attempt

This check exists to reduce accidental or careless misuse, not to create a hard privacy-invasive gate.

### 8.5 Multiple Bus Scan Suspicion

If one student scans multiple different buses in a suspiciously short period, the system should:

- flag the pattern for review
- audit the attempts
- not automatically lock the account in v1

## 9. Boarding Flow

Supported boarding modes:

- direct self-pay
- sponsored boarding
- emergency ride permit fallback

### 9.1 QR Scan Path

1. Student scans durable bus QR.
2. App decodes signed payload locally.
3. App prompts the student to select the relevant stop, with favorites surfaced first where available.
4. App runs on-device location safety check.
5. App shows warning if outside campus geofence, permission denied, or location quality is weak.
6. App shows confirmation screen.
7. Student confirms payment.
8. App sends boarding request with:
   - `Idempotency-Key`
   - signed QR payload or derived bus identity
   - selected stop
   - location-check result only
   - whether warning override occurred
9. Backend validates QR and resolves the current boardable service instance.
10. Backend runs wallet, fare, exemption, and duplicate-boarding rules.
11. Backend commits the ledger transaction and boarding event.

### 9.2 Sponsored Boarding Path

Sponsored boarding exists for the case where one connected student pays for another rider.

Rules:

- sponsored boarding still uses the same durable bus QR or numeric bus code
- the payer may add one additional rider in v1
- both riders share the same selected stop in v1
- backend must validate duplicate protection and eligibility for each rider
- backend must create one money-moving transaction for the payer and one boarding event per rider
- the whole request must commit atomically or fail atomically

### 9.3 Emergency Ride Permit Path

Emergency ride permits exist only as a bounded no-internet fallback.

Rules:

- the permit must be pre-issued while online
- the permit must be tied to student and device
- the permit must be one-time use
- the permit must still be used against a valid bus, stop selection, and service window
- the app may allow boarding locally and redeem the permit later when connectivity returns
- permit redemption failure must be audit-visible and handled through debt or permit policy, not silent data loss

### 9.4 Manual Fallback Path

Manual fallback is always available.

Flow:

1. Student chooses `Enter Bus Code`.
2. Student enters short numeric bus code.
3. App resolves the associated bus identity from the backend.
4. App prompts for the relevant stop selection.
5. App runs the same location safety check.
6. App shows the same confirmation screen.
7. Backend processes the request through the same service-instance and ledger rules.

Manual fallback must be audit-logged.

## 10. Confirmation Screen

Before money moves, the app must show a confirmation screen containing:

- bus code
- route name
- service label such as morning or evening
- current direction or leg when known
- boarding stop
- fare
- rider count and total fare when sponsoring
- service window
- expected wallet balance after charge
- location safety state if warning or override applies

The confirmation screen exists to catch:

- wrong-bus scans
- stale sticker photos
- accidental manual-code entry
- payment surprises

## 11. Boarding Window Semantics

### 11.1 Morning Service

- Separate morning service instance.
- Covers campus departure, route pickup movement, and return to campus.
- Boarding is operationally intended from anywhere along the route.

### 11.2 Evening Service

- Separate evening service instance.
- Covers campus departure, route drop movement, and return toward campus.
- Boarding is operationally intended from campus in v1.

### 11.3 Midroute Boarding

The system must not introduce a separate `boarding open` flag that blocks midroute boarding once a service is active.

If a service instance is within its valid window, boarding remains allowed subject to the configured service policy and business rules.

## 12. Dorm Room Scan Defense

The system does not assume QR secrecy forever. Students can photograph stickers.

Defense strategy in v1:

- QR proves bus identity only
- service window must be valid
- on-device location check warns about suspicious remote scans
- confirmation screen reduces accidental self-charge
- repeated suspicious scan patterns are audited
- admin can rotate a leaked sticker with a new QR version

This is intentionally a layered defense, not a single brittle trick.

## 13. Forgot to End Defense

The system must not rely on a driver remembering to end a route.

Primary defense:

- scheduled service window with automatic opening and automatic expiry

Secondary controls:

- driver `End` still available
- stale operational state can be flagged in admin tools

Because service windows auto-expire, a parked bus should not remain indefinitely boardable just because the driver forgot to act.

## 14. Admin Responsibilities

Admin capabilities:

- register buses
- assign default route in v1
- generate durable QR assets
- rotate and reissue bus QR assets
- create ad hoc service instances
- view scans using retired QR versions during grace period
- review suspicious scan patterns

Admins are the only users allowed to generate or rotate bus QR assets.

## 15. Audit Requirements

The system should capture QR and boarding audit data when complexity is reasonable.

Recommended audit fields:

- `scan_attempted_at`
- `student_id`
- `bus_id`
- `bus_code`
- `qr_version`
- `scan_source` such as `QR` or `MANUAL_CODE`
- `validation_result`
- `service_instance_id`
- `selected_stop_id`
- `boarding_mode`
- `paid_by_student_id`
- `sponsored_rider_id nullable`
- `emergency_permit_id nullable`
- `service_window_result`
- `location_check_result`
- `location_override_used`
- `manual_fallback_used`
- `duplicate_rule_result`
- `final_boarding_result`

Required audit cases:

- blocked scans outside service window
- manual fallback usage
- multiple bus scans by one student in a short period
- old QR version use during grace period

## 16. Security Requirements

- Bus QR assets must be signed server-side.
- The QR payload must include `institute_id` to reduce cross-deployment reuse risk.
- Admin-only endpoints must control generation and rotation.
- Old versions must be invalidated automatically after grace expiry.
- Sticker leakage must be recoverable by QR rotation, not by bus replacement.

## 17. Operational Monitoring

The system should track:

- boarding attempts by bus
- boarding rejections by reason
- scans outside valid service window
- manual fallback usage count
- sponsored boarding usage count
- emergency ride permit usage count
- location warning override count
- old-version QR usage count
- suspicious multiple-bus scan count
- ad hoc service instance creation count

## 18. Test Scenarios

- valid QR during active morning service
- valid QR during active evening service
- scan before early boarding window
- scan after service window plus grace
- valid QR but no current service instance
- two active service instances accidentally assigned to one bus
- old QR version during grace period
- old QR version after grace period
- manual bus-code fallback success
- manual bus-code fallback with unknown code
- sponsored boarding success
- sponsored boarding with second rider already boarded
- emergency ride permit local use and later redemption
- emergency ride permit redemption failure handling
- permission denied location flow
- outside-campus warning flow with override
- multiple bus scans by one student in a short interval
- admin-created ad hoc service instance
- driver forgets to end route but service window expires correctly
